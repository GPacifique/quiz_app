# Syllabus for Afro Students

## MATHS
- Number sense and operations (contextualized with African trade and markets)
- Algebra (patterns in African art and music)
- Geometry (African architecture and design)
- Measurement (local units, land, and resources)
- Data handling (statistics with local data)
- Problem-solving (real-life African scenarios)

## ENGLISH
- Reading comprehension (African literature and folktales)
- Writing (essays, letters, creative writing with local themes)
- Grammar and vocabulary (contextualized with African languages)
- Listening and speaking (debates, storytelling, oral traditions)

## FRENCH
- Compréhension écrite (textes africains)
- Expression écrite (lettres, récits, thèmes africains)
- Grammaire et vocabulaire (contextualisé avec les langues africaines)
- Expression orale (contes, débats, traditions orales)

## GEOGRAPHY
- African physical geography (rivers, mountains, climate)
- Human geography (cultures, migration, urbanization)
- Environmental issues (deforestation, desertification, conservation)
- Map skills (African countries, capitals, resources)

## SCIENCES
- Biology (African flora and fauna, health issues)
- Physics (energy, sound, and light in local context)
- Chemistry (local materials, water purification)
- Environmental science (sustainability, local solutions)

## HISTORY
- Ancient African civilizations (Egypt, Mali, Great Zimbabwe)
- Colonialism and independence movements
- Modern African leaders and change-makers
- Pan-Africanism and African Union
- Local and oral histories

---
This syllabus is designed to be relevant, inclusive, and empowering for Afro students, connecting academic content to their lived experiences and heritage.
